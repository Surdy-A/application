The Online registration for Diploma Programme into the Department of Local Government and Development Studies is in progress.

The programme started in 1979 as Local Government training programme under Department of Public Administration. It became a full fledged Department of its own in April, 1984.
The Diploma programme of the Department of Local Government Studies now the Department of Local Government and Development Studies, Faculty of Administration, Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife serves as a training programme funded by the Federal Government of Nigeria. The programme was (and still is being) run in three regional centers, namely, the Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria (ABU) for the North, the University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN) for the East, and the Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU) for the South West. The designed objective at early stage was for the programme to offer support, in the form of training and re-training facilities, to junior as well as mid-level local government staff to improve their performance on the job. This is to justify the efforts of the government at federal and state levels that had seen new vigour since 1976, to make Local Government (LG) a visible and effective third tier of government in Nigeriaâ€™s federal practice.

Over the years, the Diploma programme has benefited the general public and fresh secondary school graduates to pursue university education. One of such benefits include the successful Diploma graduates with Cummulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) of 3.5 and above are qualified to apply for Direct Entry (DE) admission into 200 level B.Sc. Degree programmes in the following Departments at Obafemi Awolowo University, namely: Local Government and Development Studies, Public Administration, Political Science, Entrepreneurship Studies. However, these opportunities are not limited to Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, Nigeria.
